About MandiBhavv

MandiBhavv is a comprehensive web application designed to provide real-time vegetable and fruit prices from various markets (mandis) across Rajasthan. Our mission is to empower farmers, traders, and consumers with transparent pricing information.

Key Features:
  • Real-time market prices from local mandis across Rajasthan
  • Search functionality for specific vegetables and fruits
  • City and market-specific pricing information
  • Visual presentation with product images
  • Regularly updated pricing information

How to Use MandiBhavv

Step 1: Select Your Location

From the homepage, select your state (default: Rajasthan), then choose your city from the dropdown menu.

Step 2: Select Your Nearest Mandi

After selecting your city, choose the specific market (mandi) you want to check prices for.

Step 3: Browse or Search for Products

View all available products or use the search box to find specific vegetables or fruits.

Tip: Prices are updated daily. Check back regularly for the most current information.

Understanding Agricultural Markets (Mandis)

Agricultural markets, commonly known as "Mandis" in India, are regulated marketplaces where farmers can sell their agricultural produce directly to traders, retailers, and sometimes consumers.

Key Aspects of Mandis:
  • APMC Regulated: Most mandis operate under the Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) regulations.
  • Market Yards: Physical spaces where trading occurs, often with weighing facilities and auction platforms.
  • Commission Agents: Known as "Arthiyas," they facilitate transactions between farmers and buyers.
  • Price Discovery: Prices are determined through auction or negotiation based on supply and demand.
  • Seasonal Variations: Prices fluctuate based on seasonal availability and market conditions.

Seasonal Crop Guide for Rajasthan

Understanding seasonal crop patterns helps predict price fluctuations and availability. Here's a seasonal guide for major vegetables and fruits in Rajasthan:

Vegetables:
  • Cauliflower, Cabbage, Carrot
  • Peas, Spinach, Fenugreek (Methi)
  • Radish, Turnip, Beetroot
  • Broccoli, Capsicum
Fruits:
  • Oranges, Kinnow
  • Guava, Strawberry
  • Papaya, Banana
Price Trend: Generally lower prices for winter vegetables due to abundant supply. Best time to buy leafy vegetables and root crops.

Vegetables:
  • Bottle Gourd (Lauki), Ridge Gourd (Tori)
  • Bitter Gourd (Karela), Cucumber
  • Lady Finger (Bhindi), Pumpkin
  • Tomato, Brinjal, Green Chillies
Fruits:
  • Mango, Watermelon, Muskmelon
  • Litchi, Jamun
  • Phalsa, Summer Apples
Price Trend: Higher prices for most vegetables due to water scarcity and heat. Fruits like watermelon and mango are in season and reasonably priced.

Vegetables:
  • Onion, Potato, Green Beans
  • Cluster Beans (Guar), Corn
  • Green Leafy Vegetables
  • Tinda, Pointed Gourd (Parwal)
Fruits:
  • Apples, Pears
  • Pomegranate, Custard Apple
  • Banana, Papaya
Price Trend: Fluctuating prices due to supply chain disruptions caused by rains. Leafy vegetables can be expensive, while gourds are generally affordable.
Tips for Seasonal Buying:
  • Buy vegetables and fruits in their peak season for best quality and price
  • Plan your purchase early in the morning for freshest produce
  • Compare prices between different mandis using MandiBhavv
  • Buy in bulk during peak season and preserve (if possible) for off-season use
